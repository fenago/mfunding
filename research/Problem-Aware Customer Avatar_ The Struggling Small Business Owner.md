# Problem-Aware Customer Avatar: The Struggling Small Business Owner

**Stage of Awareness:** Problem Aware

## A → Who Are They

*   **Name:** Michael “Mike” Chen
*   **Gender:** Male
*   **Job:** Owner of a small construction company (e.g., residential remodeling, roofing, or a specialized trade) with a crew of 3-5 employees.
*   **Household Income:** $75,000 - $125,000. His personal income is directly tied to the business’s performance, so it can be volatile. He prioritizes paying his employees and suppliers before himself, especially during lean periods.
*   **Marital Status:** Married with two children (one in middle school, one in high school).
*   **Education Level:** Completed some college or a trade school program but does not have a four-year degree. He values practical experience and skills over formal education.

## B → What They Do & Like

*   **Top 3 Brands they wear:** Carhartt (for work), Nike (for casual wear), and Levi’s (for everything else). He values durability, comfort, and a classic, no-nonsense style.
*   **1-2 Hobbies they have:**
    *   **DIY Home Projects:** He’s always working on something around his own house, whether it’s building a deck, finishing the basement, or landscaping the yard. It’s a way for him to relax and use his skills for his family.
    *   **Watching Sports:** He’s a dedicated fan of his local NFL and MLB teams, and enjoys watching games with friends and family.
*   **Top 5 Favorite movies:**
    1.  *The Shawshank Redemption*
    2.  *Saving Private Ryan*
    3.  *The Dark Knight*
    4.  *Goodfellas*
    5.  *The Pursuit of Happyness*
*   **Top 5 Favorite books:**
    1.  *The E-Myth Revisited* by Michael E. Gerber
    2.  *Extreme Ownership* by Jocko Willink and Leif Babin
    3.  *Rich Dad Poor Dad* by Robert T. Kiyosaki
    4.  *Good to Great* by Jim Collins
    5.  *Never Split the Difference* by Chris Voss
*   **Top 5 visited websites:**
    1.  **YouTube:** For “how-to” videos on construction techniques, tool reviews, and business advice from other contractors.
    2.  **Local News Website:** To stay updated on community events, weather, and local economic news.
    3.  **Supplier Websites:** (e.g., Home Depot Pro, Lowe’s for Pros, or a local lumber yard) to check material prices and availability.
    4.  **Facebook:** To connect with other local business owners, find new hires through community groups, and see what his friends and family are up to.
    5.  **ESPN:** To follow his favorite sports teams.
*   **Top 5 relevant social media influencers:**
    1.  **Gary Vaynerchuk (@garyvee):** He’s drawn to Gary’s hustle mentality and no-excuses approach to business.
    2.  **Grant Cardone (@grantcardone):** He’s intrigued by Grant’s focus on sales, marketing, and building a massive business empire.
    3.  **Patrick Bet-David (@patrickbetdavid):** He appreciates Patrick’s practical business advice and interviews with successful entrepreneurs on Valuetainment.
    4.  **The Angry Contractor (or similar construction-focused influencer):** He follows accounts that share humorous and relatable content about the daily frustrations of running a construction business.
    5.  **A local business news page or chamber of commerce account:** To stay connected with the local business community.

## C → Why Are They

*   **Main Personality Traits:**
    *   **Resilient:** He’s been knocked down before but always gets back up. He sees challenges as a part of the game.
    *   **Pragmatic:** He’s a no-nonsense, practical thinker who focuses on what works in the real world, not just on paper.
    *   **Hard-working:** He’s often the first one on the job site and the last one to leave. He’s not afraid to get his hands dirty.
    *   **Independent:** He prides himself on being his own boss and building something from the ground up.
    *   **Loyal:** He’s fiercely loyal to his family, his employees, and his best customers.
*   **5 Major Values They Hold:**
    1.  **Integrity:** Doing the job right and standing by his work.
    2.  **Family:** Providing for his family is his primary motivation.
    3.  **Freedom:** The ability to control his own schedule and destiny.
    4.  **Craftsmanship:** Taking pride in the quality of his work.
    5.  **Community:** He values his local reputation and relationships.
*   **2 Major Life Victories:**
    1.  **Starting his own business:** Leaving a stable job to go out on his own was a huge risk, but he’s proud that he built a company that supports his family and his employees.
    2.  **Buying his first home:** It was a major milestone that represented stability and success for his family.
*   **2 Major Life Failures:**
    1.  **A past business partnership that went sour:** He lost money and a friendship, which made him more cautious about trusting others in business.
    2.  **Underbidding a large job early in his career:** He ended up losing money on the project and learned a hard lesson about accurately estimating costs and managing cash flow.

## D → Smart Market Questions

*   **What keeps them awake at night, eyes open, staring at the ceiling:** “Making payroll. If we have a slow month or a client pays late, how am I going to make sure my guys get paid? They have families to feed, too.” He also worries about a key piece of equipment breaking down and not having the cash on hand to fix or replace it, bringing his entire operation to a halt.
*   **What are they secretly afraid of in life:** He’s terrified of having to close the business and go back to working for someone else. He sees it as a personal failure and a step backward for his family. He’s also afraid of his kids seeing him as a failure.
*   **What are they angry about, and who are they angry at:** He’s angry at the banks. “They’ll give you an umbrella when it’s sunny, and take it away when it starts to rain.” He’s frustrated with the endless paperwork, the unrealistic requirements, and the slow, bureaucratic process, only to be told “no” because his personal credit score isn’t perfect or he doesn’t have enough collateral.
*   **Top 3 frustrations they feel every day:**
    1.  **Late-paying clients:** “I did the work, why can’t they just pay me on time?”
    2.  **Unpredictable cash flow:** The constant cycle of feast or famine. One month he’s flush with cash, the next he’s scrambling to cover expenses.
    3.  **Wearing too many hats:** He’s the CEO, the head of sales, the project manager, and sometimes, an extra laborer. He’s stretched thin and doesn’t have enough time to focus on growing the business.
*   **Biggest secret desire in life:** To build a business that runs without him. He dreams of being able to take a two-week vacation with his family without constantly checking his phone or worrying about the business falling apart.
*   **Built-in bias to how they make decisions:** He’s biased towards action and tangible results. He trusts his gut and what he can see and touch more than abstract theories or complex spreadsheets. He also relies heavily on recommendations from other business owners he trusts.
*   **Common words or language unique to them:** “Cash is king,” “measure twice, cut once,” “the bottom line,” “get ‘er done,” “it is what it is,” “boots on the ground.”
*   **Top 3 complaints about existing solutions:**
    1.  **Banks are too slow:** “By the time they approve the loan, the opportunity is gone.”
    2.  **Banks have impossible standards:** “You need a perfect credit score, a mountain of paperwork, and a 20-year-old business to even get a meeting.”
    3.  **Banks don’t understand my business:** “They just see a small construction company and think it’s too risky. They don’t see the contracts I have lined up or the demand for my work.”

## E → Going Deep

*   **Top 3 Dominant Negative Emotions:**
    1.  **Anxiety:** The constant, low-level hum of worry about cash flow, payroll, and the future of the business.
    2.  **Frustration:** A feeling of being stuck and powerless against slow-paying clients and rigid financial institutions.
    3.  **Exhaustion:** The mental and physical toll of working long hours and carrying the weight of the business on his shoulders.
*   **Top 3 Dominant Positive Emotions From Solving This Problem:**
    1.  **Relief:** The immediate release of pressure that comes from knowing he can make payroll and cover his expenses.
    2.  **Confidence:** The feeling of being in control of his business and his destiny.
    3.  **Optimism:** The ability to look to the future and focus on growth instead of just survival.
*   **Top 3 Beliefs They Hold About The World:**
    1.  “Hard work pays off, but sometimes it’s not enough.”
    2.  “You have to be a little bit of a fighter to make it in this world.”
    3.  “Your reputation is everything.”
*   **Biggest Lifestyle Desire:** To have more time and less stress. He wants to be able to coach his son’s baseball team, attend his daughter’s school events, and have dinner with his family without being distracted by work.

## F → Purchasing Habits

*   **Top 3 Decision Triggers:**
    1.  **Urgency:** A critical piece of equipment breaks down, or he needs to make a big upfront materials purchase for a new job.
    2.  **Opportunity:** A chance to buy a competitor’s equipment at a discount, or the ability to take on a larger, more profitable job if he had the capital.
    3.  **Peer Recommendation:** Hearing from another contractor that they had a good experience with a particular lender or service.
*   **Prior Purchases For This Pain:** He’s likely used personal credit cards to cover business expenses in a pinch, or taken out a small, high-interest personal loan. He may have also tried to get a line of credit from his bank and been denied.
*   **Price Tolerance For Offer:** He’s less sensitive to the interest rate and more focused on the speed and ease of getting the funds. He understands that he’ll pay a premium for convenience and is willing to do so if it solves his immediate problem. He thinks in terms of the cost of *not* having the money (e.g., “If I don’t get this $20,000, I’ll lose a $100,000 job”).
*   **Time Horizon Of Solution:** He needs the money *now*. He’s looking for a solution that can get him funded in days, not weeks or months.

## G → Primary Wants

*   **Wants to gain:**
    *   Peace of mind
    *   Control over his cash flow
    *   The ability to seize opportunities
*   **Wants to be:**
    *   Seen as a successful and reliable business owner
    *   A good provider for his family
    *   Respected in his community
*   **Wants to do:**
    *   Focus on growing his business, not just managing it
    *   Take on bigger and better jobs
    *   Spend more quality time with his family
*   **Wants to save:**
    *   Time (by avoiding lengthy application processes)
    *   His business from a cash flow crisis
    *   His personal credit from being over-leveraged
*   **Wants to avoid:**
    *   Missing payroll
    *   Turning down profitable jobs
    *   The embarrassment of having to close his business

## H → Empathy Map

*   **Seeing:** Late payment notices, his crew looking to him for answers, his wife’s worried expression, his competitors with newer equipment, opportunities passing him by.
*   **Thinking:** “How am I going to get through this month? Why is it so hard to get a simple loan? I’m a good businessman, I just need a little help. I can’t let my guys down. I can’t let my family down.”
*   **Hearing:** His banker telling him “no,” his wife asking about their finances, his foreman telling him a piece of equipment is down, a podcast from Gary Vee telling him to “crush it.”
*   **Feeling:** Stressed, anxious, frustrated, overwhelmed, but also determined and hopeful.
*   **Saying:** “We’ll figure it out, we always do.” “I just need a little breathing room.” “The bank is useless.” “I’m working on it.”
*   **Doing:** Juggling multiple jobs, chasing down invoices, looking for financing options online late at night, trying to put on a brave face for his family and employees.
