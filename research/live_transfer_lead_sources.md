## 2. 🔴 The biggest risk isn't lead quality — it's TCPA, and it lands on YOU

This was the best-sourced part of the research (multiple law firms + FCC/FTC primary sources), and it matters more than which vendor you pick.

- **"TCPA-compliant lead" is a myth.** As the business that *dials* the lead, **you are the caller/brand** — you bear **direct liability** if the consent is invalid, and you can *also* be held **vicariously liable for the vendor's** violations (agency/ratification). An indemnification clause shifts *cost*, not *legal liability*.
- **Damages:** **$500 per violation, up to $1,500 for willful**, per call/text, **no aggregate cap, 4-year statute of limitations.** ~80% of TCPA suits are class actions.
- **New FCC rule (effective Apr 11, 2025):** you must honor an opt-out/revocation **within 10 business days**, and a consumer can revoke by **any reasonable method** (verbal, text, email — "stop," "revoke," "opt-out"). You cannot force one exclusive channel.
- **Real bad actors exist:** the FTC **permanently banned RAM Capital Funding** and its owner from the MCA industry and ordered **$675,000** in redress. The FTC's "Strictly Business" perspective explicitly flags aggressive/misleading MCA marketing.

**Implication:** any vendor that can't produce the **opt-in consent chain** (source, timestamp, exact opt-in language) for a transferred lead is handing you their legal risk. That's a hard gate, not a nice-to-have.

## 3. Vetting checklist — run this BEFORE paying any new vendor

Reject the vendor if they fail any of these:

- [ ] **Consent chain on demand.** Can they show, per lead, the opt-in **source + timestamp + exact language**? (No → walk away — that's your TCPA exposure.)
- [ ] **Exclusivity in writing.** Is the *live-transfer* product **truly exclusive** (one buyer), stated in the contract — not "exclusive" in the pitch and "semi-exclusive" in the fine print?
- [ ] **Criteria-match-rate history.** Will they provide historical % of transfers that met the filter (ideally contract-committed)? Vague "high quality" = no.
- [ ] **Transfer speed in MINUTES, not hours.** Target: merchant answers within seconds, median speed-to-transfer **under ~5 minutes.** Hours = a warmed-over data lead re-dialed.
- [ ] **Real-time QA coverage.** Target **95–100%** of calls monitored, not "manual QA on 2–5%."
- [ ] **Return/replacement policy in writing** with concrete conditions (Synergy's 9-condition list is a decent benchmark).
- [ ] **Reputation:** independent reviews on **deBanked, DailyFunder forums, Trustpilot, BBB** — patterns of complaints or *no professional online presence at all* is an FTC-recognized red flag.
- [ ] **US-based, named call center** (offshore boiler rooms drive the spoofed-transfer and TCPA problems).

## 4. The paid TEST BATCH — how to spend a little to learn a lot

Never scale a vendor on their pitch. Buy a small batch and measure against your **Synergy baseline (~$45/transfer)**. A real DailyFunder buyer test of a live-transfer vendor found **only 11 of 25 transfers (~44%) met a basic quality bar** and rated it 5/10 — proof that a test routinely exposes what marketing hides.

**Protocol:**
1. **Sample size:** 25–50 transfers (enough to see a pattern; small enough to cap loss ≈ $1,000–$2,500).
2. **Define YOUR quality bar first** (before the test): e.g. owner on call + verifiably $15K+/mo + not already funded + not in default.
3. **Track these five metrics** (you already have the campaign KPI tooling in the app for this — set up a campaign code per vendor):
   - **Connect rate** — % where the owner was actually live
   - **Qualified rate** — % that met YOUR bar (the honest number; expect ~40–60%)
   - **Application rate** — % that submitted
   - **Close/fund rate** — % funded
   - **Cost per funded deal** — the only number that matters
4. **Pass/fail thresholds** (tune to your economics): qualified rate **≥ 50%**, and **cost-per-funded ≤ your Synergy cost-per-funded.** If a vendor can't beat or match what you already have, don't scale it.
5. **Log every bad transfer against the return policy** during the test — it's also how you find out whether the policy is honored in practice.

## 5. Where MCA brokers actually find & vet vendors

- **deBanked** (debanked.com) — the industry publication; vendor directory + articles.
- **DailyFunder forums** (dailyfunder.com) — brokers post real, unvarnished vendor reviews (the 44% test above came from here).
- **Trustpilot / Google / BBB** — for complaint patterns, not for the star average.
- **Referrals from other ISOs** you trust — the highest-signal source; a vendor a funding ISO actively re-buys from beats any list.
