## 1. The North Star — what "$1M in 6 months" actually requires

You have **two revenue engines**, and the plan only works if you run **both**:

| Engine | Avg revenue per deal (to the company) | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **MCA origination** | **~$4,000** (8 pts on a $50K advance, founder-closed, no split) | Fast cash — commission paid ~5 business days after the funder funds |
| **VCF debt relief** (white-label) | **~$5,000–$20,000 per file** (≈10% of enrolled debt; $50K–$200K typical) | Big-ticket — but **paid over the client's repayment term, not upfront** |

**The blunt truth about the goal.** $1,000,000 ÷ $4,000 = **250 funded MCA deals in 6 months (~42/mo)** — not realistic for a startup from a standing start. But **blended with VCF debt relief, $1M in *booked* commissions is reachable**, because one debt-relief file is worth 1.25–5 MCA deals. The catch: **VCF commission is back-loaded** (it drips over the repayment term), so **$1M booked ≠ $1M cash-in-hand by month 6**. Plan to *contract* ~$1M and *collect* a meaningful fraction within the 6 months, with the rest annuitizing after.

**Realistic base case vs. stretch (booked revenue):**

| | Month 1 | Month 2 | Month 3 | Month 4 | Month 5 | Month 6 | 6-mo total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MCA funded deals | 2 | 4 | 6 | 8 | 10 | 12 | **42 deals → ~$168K** |
| VCF debt-relief files | 1 | 3 | 5 | 7 | 9 | 11 | **36 files → ~$360K** (at $10K avg) |
| **Booked revenue/mo** | ~$18K | ~$46K | ~$74K | ~$102K | ~$130K | ~$158K | **~$528K base** |
| **Stretch** (higher close + 2nd closer + bigger VCF files @ $14K) | — | — | — | — | — | — | **~$950K–$1.1M** |

**Read this honestly:** the **base case is ~$500–550K booked**; **$1M is the stretch** and it hinges on (a) hiring a second closer by month 3–4, (b) VCF files averaging $12–14K not $10K, and (c) hitting the upper end of close rates. The single biggest lever to $1M is **VCF debt relief**, not MCA origination — because the per-file commission is 3–5× larger. **Lead with debt relief from your own book + stacked-UCC lists, fund the pipeline with live transfers, and build the compounding channels (SEO/organic/social) underneath.**

**The Golden Ratio (your CLAUDE.md metric):** keep **cost-per-funded-deal < $1,500** while **commission > $4,000**. Every channel below is judged against that bar.

**Vendor vetting checklist (the research found rampant "shared-sold-as-exclusive" fraud — most vendors buy a lead for ~$50 and resell to 10–20 brokers):**
1. **Pay-only-for-qualified + written replacement guarantee** (MCA Leadz, MCA Leads Hub both do this).
2. **Demand call recordings + a reporting dashboard** to audit qualification.
3. **Require written TCPA 1:1 consent provenance** (timestamps/IP/opt-in copy) and **DNC + known-litigator scrub** — one real broker test received a *professional TCPA plaintiff* among 30 transfers.
4. **Split a small test across 3 vendors before committing**; track **cost-per-funded-deal, not cost-per-lead**.
5. **Qualify on bank deposits, not gross revenue** (deals die in underwriting on low deposits).
6. **Avoid $26–$30 "too cheap" transfers** — almost always recycled/shared.

### Unit economics — your $3,000 live-transfer test

**Assumptions (labeled):** blended **$75/transfer** (mid-market exclusive); **company nets $4,000** per funded deal (8 pts on $50K, founder-closed, no split — *your target; realistic range $2,000–$4,000, see note*). $3,000 ÷ $75 = **40 transfers.**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **3%** (realistic floor — break-even underwriting) | 1.2 | $4,800 | **+$1,800** | $2,500 | 1.6× |

| **8%** (Month 1–3 target w/ good closer + instant pickup) | 3.2 | $12,800 | **+$9,800** | $938 | 4.3× |
| **12%** (mature, tight follow-up) | 4.8 | $19,200 | **+$16,200** | $625 | 6.4× |

**Same $3,000 at different transfer prices (at 8% close, $4,000/deal):**

| Price/transfer | Transfers | Funded @8% | Revenue | Net |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $50 | 60 | 4.8 | $19,200 | +$16,200 |
| $75 | 40 | 3.2 | $12,800 | +$9,800 |
| $100 | 30 | 2.4 | $9,600 | +$6,600 |
| $150 | 20 | 1.6 | $6,400 | +$3,400 |

**Reality checks from real broker data (not vendor claims):**
- **Underwrite to ~3% funded as your floor.** Vendor "15–35% close" claims are marketing. A real 30-transfer test (Enablement Data) produced **1 funded deal that defaulted in week 1** (~3% funded, 0% profitable). At 5–12% you need **experienced closers, <60-second pickup, and structured follow-up**.
- **The #1 controllable leak is follow-up,** not lead quality — in that test, **8 of 30 were "app out but never submitted."** This maps exactly to your **Sequence A (doc-collection)** priority. Win here and your close rate moves from 3% → 8%+.
- **Commission caveat:** Research shows **typical direct-broker commission is 1–4 points**, and your 8-pt/$4,000 model is **top-of-range** — achievable as a direct ISO with strong funder relationships and ~1.4 factor sells, but if you're sub-broking under another ISO early, model **$2,000–$2,800/deal** and the tables above roughly halve.

**Verdict:** Live transfers are your **fastest, most predictable MCA origination channel.** Spend the $3,000 as **3 × $1,000 split tests** across (e.g.) MCA Leadz, MCA Leads Hub, and Lead Slaps; kill the losers; double down on cost-per-funded-deal winners. Expect to see true ROI on a **~90-day lag** (fund → renewal).

## 4. CHANNEL 2 — Lead Lists & Data (cheap MCA volume + renewals/stacking)

Three sub-channels, cheapest cost-per-record to highest intent:

| Source | Cost / record | Intent | Realistic funded rate | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold B2B data** (industry/rev/geo) | $0.02–$0.20 (Lead Tycoons $0.10 mobile) | Lowest (firmographic only) | ~0.5–2% | Pipeline building |
| **Aged MCA leads** | $0.05–$5 (validated $1.75–$5) | Medium (prior inquiry) | 1–5% | Scale + GHL nurture |
| **UCC filing data** | **$0.175** (Lead Tycoons $175/1,000) – $0.75 | **Medium-high (proven borrowers)** | Best cold-list ratio | **Renewals, refinance, STACKING** |

**UCC data is the highest-ROI cold list for an MCA shop.** A UCC-1 is filed when a merchant takes an advance — so the list is *proven borrowers*, and the secured-party field tells you **which funder** is on the deal (deBanked publishes a funder-alias list). Vendors: **Lead Tycoons** ($175/1,000), **Klover Data** (segment by filing date/amount/state — how you isolate stacked accounts), **NAICS/Mailinglists.com**, **DataMan Group**, **Synergy Direct**, **The Leads Warehouse**.
> Note: the oft-quoted *"60–70% of UCC merchants want more capital"* is **marketing lore**, not a verified stat — use it directionally.

### Unit economics — a 5,000-record UCC cold-dial campaign (illustrative)

**Assumptions:** 5,000 UCC records @ $0.175 = **$875 data**. Cold dial. Connect ~25–30%, qualify a fraction, fund ~1–2% of records.

| Funded rate on records | Funded MCA deals | Revenue (@$4,000) | Data cost | Gross (ex-labor) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1% | 50 | $200,000 | $875 | huge — **but labor-bound** |
| 0.5% | 25 | $100,000 | $875 | — |

**The honest constraint is labor, not data cost.** 5,000 dials needs a dialer + reps + DNC/litigator scrub (+10% vendor add-on) + A2P 10DLC for SMS. Data is pennies; the cost-per-funded-deal is mostly **your team's time**. This is why it's a **parallel, always-on cheap channel feeding GHL nurture (Sequences B/C/F)**, not your day-one focus.

**Compliance (binding):** TCPA is unsettled in 2026 (the 1:1 consent rule was vacated/removed; circuit split). Mandatory regardless: **DNC + litigator scrub on every list**, **manual dialing to cell numbers** (auto-dialers/RVM to cells are the high-liability acts), **A2P 10DLC for SMS**, documented consent per source. Exposure is **$500–$1,500 per call/text** — this is the channel's main financial risk. And per your compliance rules: **never say "loan" for MCA** — "funding/working capital/advance."

## 5. CHANNEL 3 — SEO / Organic Search (the compounding moat)

**Set expectations now: SEO produces ~0 leads in months 1–3, real volume at 6–12 months, and competes for money keywords at 12–18 months.** Finance is one of the most expensive, most adversarial niches online (Lendio/NerdWallet/Bankrate/Forbes own page 1).

**CPC reality (why this niche is brutal):** the *blended* finance CPC is ~$3.46, but **MCA/loan money terms run $15–$45/click** (WordStream: "loans" top CPC $44.28, "credit" $36.06). That's 7–10× the all-industry average — and it's the same auction your Google Ads will pay into.

**The winnable strategy for a new domain — don't chase head terms; win the long tail:**

| Theme | Est. CPC | Difficulty | Play |
|---|---|---|---|
| Geo / city ("business funding Indianapolis") | $6–$20 | Medium | **Hand-built pages for your 6 metros** |
| Industry ("trucking/restaurant funding") | $8–$22 | Medium | Ties to your metros' industries |
| Spanish ("capital de trabajo") | $4–$15 | Low-med | **Underserved — your Phoenix/Miami/Sac Hispanic focus** |
| Debt relief ("MCA debt relief," "reverse consolidation") | $15–$30 | Med-high | **Distressed intent → feeds VCF** |
| Informational ("what is an MCA," calculators) | $2–$8 | Medium | Cheap, builds authority, retargeting fuel |

**Cost/timeline:** outsourced **$2,500–$5,000/mo** (finance is YMYL — cheap tier underperforms), or in-house content lead + AI drafting + freelance links. Build ~15–25 cornerstone pages first, then **4–8 quality pages/mo**. **Do NOT mass-generate thin city pages** — Google's March 2026 update explicitly targets "scaled content abuse."

**Realistic organic lead projection:**

**Verdict:** Start day one (calculators, "what is an MCA," 6 city pages, debt-relief cluster, GBP), but **do not budget SEO as a 6-month revenue source** — it's your month-9-and-beyond engine. Note: **Local Services Ads are NOT available for lending/MCA** — don't plan around them.

## 6. CHANNEL 4 — Social Media Marketing

**The hard constraint (read first):** **Meta prohibits "short-term loans of 90 days or less"** — and an MCA repaid via daily/weekly remittance reads exactly like that to reviewers. Since Jan 2025, all U.S. financial advertisers must use Meta's **Financial Products Special Ad Category**, which **removes age/ZIP/interest targeting and Lookalikes** — you can't precisely target "business owners." **Account disablement is a *when*, not *if*.** Run paid social on a **disposable Business Manager**, never your only account.

| Platform | Fit | Policy risk | Est. CPL | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Meta retargeting** | High | Lower (1st-party) | ~$15–$30 | **Re-convert your traffic + dead leads** |
| **Meta cold lead-gen** | Volume, low intent | **HIGH** | $30–$60 (finance) | Optional volume test |
| **LinkedIn** | High intent, expensive | Medium | $50–$130 | **Big loans + Sub-ISO recruiting** |
| **TikTok/IG Reels (organic)** | **Best organic fit** | Low (organic) | ~$0 | **Founder education video** |
| **X** | Weak | — | — | Skip |

### Unit economics — paid social vs. the bar

| Channel | Qualified-lead cost | Funded @8% | **Cost/funded deal** | vs. $1,500 target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Meta retargeting** | ~$25–$45 | warm, higher close | **~$400–$900** | ✅ **Best paid-social ROI** |
| Meta cold lead-gen | ~$90–$180 | 1 per ~12 | ~$1,100–$2,200 | ⚠️ Borderline + volatile |
| LinkedIn lead gen | $100–$260 | low MCA close | ~$2,500–$5,000+ | ❌ Too costly for $50K MCA |

**Verdict:**
- **Best paid move = retargeting.** Upload your CRM/aged-lead lists as **Custom Audiences** (the sanctioned workaround under Special Ad Category) and retarget apply.mfunding.com abandoners + dead leads. Retargeted users convert ~70% more, at 30–60% cheaper clicks. This **pairs perfectly with your Sequence F mass-reactivation** — same dead list, near-zero incremental cost.
- **Best durable move = organic founder video** (TikTok + Reels + Shorts, 3–5/week: "what funders look at on your bank statements," "signs you're over-leveraged on advances") + **LinkedIn thought leadership/outbound** (also your Sub-ISO recruiting channel). 3–6 month compounding play.
- **Debt relief on social:** education + retargeting only — **not** cold paid (FTC is actively enforcing against deceptive debt-relief ads; avoid "eliminate your debt" claims).

## 7. CHANNEL 5 — VCF Debt Relief (your biggest-ticket lever, white-label)

You're a **white-label partner of Value Capital Funding**, offering distressed merchants: (a) attorney-led **MCA debt restructuring** (no new loan, lowers payments 50–75%, $50K+ debt, no FICO min) and (b) **FDIC bank term-loan refinancing**. This is where the **$5,000–$20,000-per-file** commissions live.

### Where the leads come from (ranked)

1. **YOUR OWN BOOK (start here, ~$0 cost).** Every declined MCA app, every funded-but-struggling/stacked client. The entire ISO debt-relief playbook (Delancey Street, MCAShield) is built on exactly this: "every broker has a list of past merchants who didn't make it." **Highest conversion, zero data cost — mine your CRM this week.**
2. **Stacked-UCC cold lists.** Businesses with **multiple UCC-1 filings** = over-leveraged = distressed. Buy raw UCC data (Klover, Synergy, Lead Tycoons, The Leads Warehouse), filter for **recent + multiple filings**, run multi-position de-dupe yourself (no clean turnkey "stacked-only" file exists at a public price).
3. SMS/RVM + direct mail to scrubbed stacked lists; **PPC** on "MCA debt relief / reverse consolidation / stop MCA payments" (test on target-CPA, not CPC).

### Unit economics — a 10,000-record stacked-UCC campaign

**Assumptions (modeled; only the $60–160 cost-per-booked-appointment is an external benchmark):**

| Stage | Conversion | From 10,000 records |
|---|---|---|
| Data cost | $0.02–$0.75/rec | **$200–$7,500** |
| Reached (connect) | 25–35% | ~3,000 |
| Interested in relief | 8–15% | ~300–450 |
| Qualified consult ($50K+ debt, hardship, PG) | 30–40% | ~100–160 |
| **Signed clients** | 20–35% | **~25–50** |
| **Net: record → signed** | **~0.3–0.5%** | **25–50 clients** |

**Commission on that cohort:** 25–50 files × **$5,000–$20,000** (≈10% of $50K–$200K enrolled debt) = **$125K–$1M+ in booked commission** on $200–$7,500 of data. Even the pessimistic end (25 files × $5K = $125K on $7,500 data + labor) is **strongly positive**.

> **Three caveats you must respect:**
> 1. **Commission is back-loaded** — it drips over the client's repayment term, not as a 5-day lump sum like MCA. Great annuity, slow cash. **Get VCF's exact rate card + persistency/clawback terms in writing before you scale spend.**
> 2. **Compliance gray area:** MCA debt is *commercial*, so the FTC consumer advance-fee ban isn't squarely on point (which is why upfront-fee shops exist) — but **TCPA/DNC apply to B2B cell calls/SMS**, "substantial assistance" liability means you must **vet VCF's compliance**, and all claims ("50–75% reduction," "90% acceptance") must be substantiated. **Lead with "no upfront fees."**
> 3. **Don't conflate "reverse consolidation" with relief** — independent sources warn it *adds* debt. VCF's restructuring + FDIC refinance is the more defensible positioning; market it that way.

**Verdict:** **This is your fastest path to big revenue and the key to the $1M stretch.** Start with your own book (week 1), layer stacked-UCC cold lists (week 2–4). One signed file ≈ 1.25–5 MCA deals.

- **$1M in 6 months is a stretch, and "booked ≠ collected"** — VCF commissions annuitize. Base case is ~$500–550K booked; $1M needs the stretch conditions in §1.

- **Meta ad disablement is routine** for MCA — never depend on a single ad account.
- **Vendor fraud is rampant** — "exclusive" leads are often resold to 10–20 brokers; vet hard, track cost-per-funded-deal.
- **Compliance language:** MCA is never a "loan"; debt-relief claims must be substantiated; lead with "no upfront fees."
