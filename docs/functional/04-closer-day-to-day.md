# 4. Day-to-Day for a Closer

You have four screens. Learn these and you know the job.

| Screen | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Revenue Playbooks** (`/admin/playbooks`) | **Your home screen.** My Day sits at the top; the guided step-by-step close sits underneath. |
| **Deals** (`/admin/deals`) | The full list of your deals. Filters, search, the deal detail page. |
| **My Earnings** (`/admin/my-earnings`) | Every dollar you've made and every dollar still on the table. |
| **Closer Comp** (`/admin/closer-comp`) | The comp plan + a calculator to model your own take-home. |

---

## 4.1 My Day — the first thing you look at

My Day is the ranked work queue at the top of the Revenue Playbook. It answers one question:
**what needs me next?** It refreshes itself constantly — a lead created seconds ago pops in without
a reload — and a live countdown ticks on the hot ones.

It is split into **two lanes**.

### Lane 1 — 📎 Follow-up · chasing stips
*Deals already in motion. Chase the docs, the replies, the offers.*

### Lane 2 — 🆕 New work · first touch
*Brand-new and real-time leads. Make first contact.*

**Follow-up sits on top — unless there is a 🔴 CALL NOW card**, in which case new work jumps to the
top, because nothing outranks a merchant sitting on the phone waiting for you.

### What pushes a deal into each lane, in exact priority order

| Priority | Card | Lane | What it means |
|:--:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 🔴 **LIVE TRANSFER / REAL-TIME — CALL NOW** | New work | A hot lead with a **live countdown** in the corner. When the clock runs out it flips to *"SLA MISSED — call anyway."* |
| 2 | 💬 **Funder replied** | Follow-up | A funder responded and the deal is still parked in Submitted. Pull the offer in. |
| 3 | 💬 **Merchant replied** | Follow-up | They wrote back in the last 3 days. The card shows you a summary of what they said. |
| 4 | 📄 **Present offers** | Follow-up | An offer has been in hand 24h+ and hasn't been presented. |
| 5 | 📎 **Promised statements N days ago** | Follow-up | **The loudest chase signal there is.** They named a date and it passed. Call and hold them to it. |
| 6 | ⏰ **Docs stale** | Follow-up | A docs stage hasn't moved in 3+ days. |
| 7 | 📎 **Statements promised TODAY** | Follow-up | They said today. Check in before the day gets away. |
| 8 | 📤 **Nudge funders** | Follow-up | Submitted 48h+ ago and every funder is still silent. |
| 9 | 🌤️ **Warm lead — call now** | New work | A purchased/qualified lead. These decay fast. |
| 10 | 🆕 **New lead — make first contact** | New work | Never contacted. Appears **immediately** on creation. |
| 11 | 📎 **Statements due in N days** | Follow-up | They committed to a future date. Nothing to chase yet — keep it warm. |
| 12 | ☎️ 📋 ✍️ 📎 🤝 📝 **stage nudges** | Follow-up | *Qualify this lead · Send the application · Chase the signed app · Collect the stips · Close the offer · Push to funding.* |
| 13 | 🔧 **In progress** | Follow-up | Any other open deal. |

**Nothing open ever disappears from My Day.** If a deal is alive, it has a card.

Cards are also colour-coded by **how the lead arrived** (live transfer = red, web form = blue, cold
email = purple, renewal = green…) and show the deal size at a glance ("$50K ask").

**Overdue flags:** a funder or merchant reply goes red after **4 hours**. A new or warm lead goes
red after **1 hour**. A stage nudge goes red after a **full business day**.

### How to use it

1. Open **Revenue Playbooks**. Scan My Day top to bottom.
2. **Click a card.** It loads that merchant into the playbook below *and* switches to the right
   playbook for how that lead arrived. You are now working that deal.
3. Do the thing the card told you to do.
4. Go back to the top.

That's the day.

## 4.2 The Revenue Playbook — the guided close

Underneath My Day is the playbook: the exact steps for the deal you have loaded. There is a
separate playbook per lead type, because a live transfer is not worked like a cold-email reply:

| Playbook | For |
|---|---|
| **Website Lead → Funded** | Someone filled out the form on the site. |
| **Live Transfer Call** | A vendor has a merchant on the phone for you *right now*. |
| **Real-Time Lead → Funded** | A hot pre-qualified lead on a countdown. |
| **Purchased Web Lead → Funded** | Verify their numbers, don't re-interrogate them. |
| **Aged Live Transfer → Funded** | "We connected a while back…" — re-confirm everything, it's months old. |
| **Cold Campaign Reply → Funded** | They replied to a cold email. They're at their desk — move in 15 minutes. |
| **Renewal → Second Advance** | A funded merchant crossing paydown. |
| **VCF Debt Relief** | A distressed merchant. Different pipeline, different pitch. |

Clicking a My Day card opens **the playbook that matches how that lead arrived** — you never have
to pick.

### What each step gives you

- **Do** — the numbered actions, in order.
- **Say** — an actual script. Read it if you need to. It's written to be compliant.
- **Fields** — inline capture. You type the merchant's numbers **right there** while you're on the
  phone with them. It saves straight to the deal. No hunting for another screen.
- **Collect** — the document checklist for that step. Tick things off as they land.
- **The button** — the step's action. Hitting it does the real work *and* advances the stage.

### The step that matters most: sending the application

One button. It moves the deal to **App Sent**, pushes it to the CRM, and emails the merchant three
things at once:

1. The **Merchant Funding Application** to complete and e-sign — the full application funders
   accept, filled and signed in one flow.
2. The **Broker Compensation Disclosure** to e-sign.
3. A secure link to upload their **last 4 months of bank statements, photo ID, and voided check**.

**Optional white-glove:** *before* you send, you can open their contact and type their details in
yourself as they read them to you — SSN, DOB, driver's licence, address, bank. Then the application
arrives **pre-filled** and all they do is tap to sign. It converts dramatically better.

> **Stay on the line until they confirm the email landed.** If it isn't in their inbox in 60
> seconds it's in spam. Have them drag it to the inbox and reply "got it." That reply is what
> trains their mail provider to trust us — every merchant who does it makes the next send land
> cleaner for the next merchant.

### The step that makes or breaks your month: the bank statements

Statements are where deals go to die. Two rules:

1. **Ask for a date.** Don't say "whenever you get a chance." Say *"today, or tomorrow?"*, then
   type the day they name into **"Statements promised by."**
2. That date is now your chase clock. If it passes with no statements, My Day puts the deal at the
   **top of your follow-up lane** with a red badge. You will not forget it, because the system
   won't let you.

### Run the underwriter before you burn submissions

Once the statements are in, run the **internal AI underwriter** from the docs step. It **reads the
actual statements** and hands you:

- their **true** revenue vs. what they *told* you (and the padding between the two),
- NSF count and negative days,
- how many **open MCA positions** they already have and what the existing daily debit is,
- **what they can actually afford** — max sustainable daily/weekly payment and advance size,
- a risk rating and a plain-English narrative.

Read that **before** you submit. It is the difference between a smart submission and five wasted
declines.

## 4.3 The AI deal assistant — use it on the call

Bottom-right of the playbook there's a floating **"Ask about this deal"** panel. It is scoped to
the **one deal you have loaded** and it knows that deal's entire record:

- the merchant and their financials,
- the stage and the doc checklist — **what's in and what's missing**,
- **every funder it went to and exactly what each one said**,
- the underwriting run,
- the full activity history.

It answers **only from the deal's record**. If it isn't on the deal, it tells you so — it will not
make something up.

**The use case is literal: a funder is on the phone asking you for something and you need the
answer now, without switching tabs.** Answers are deliberately short, because a wall of text is
useless mid-call.

One-tap questions:
- *"What is the funder still waiting on?"*
- *"What stips are missing?"*
- *"Where is this deal in the pipeline?"*
- *"Summarize this merchant for me."*

Or just type: *"The funder's asking for something — what is it?"*

Switching to another deal clears the conversation. It never carries context from one merchant to
another.

## 4.4 My Earnings — your money, live

**Only your deals appear here.** Nobody else's. Your three split rates are pinned at the top of the
page so you always know exactly what you're on.

### Five tiles across the top

| Tile | What it is |
|---|---|
| ⭐ **Paid to date** | Money already sent to you. |
| **Approved** | Signed off. Payout goes out within 5 business days of the funder paying us. |
| **Pending** | The deal funded, the commission is booked, it's waiting on the owner's sign-off. |
| **On hold** | The payout is paused. **The reason is written on the row.** |
| ⭐ **On the table** | Your open deals, priced. **This is a projection, not money.** |

### Section 1 — "On the table" (projection)

Every open deal assigned to you, priced off the **amount requested**, with the math shown on every
single row:

> **$50,000 × 8 pts = $4,000 pool × 30% your split = $1,200 to you**

Nothing here is earned. It becomes real when the deal funds and the funder pays.
No amount on the deal yet? Add it, and the projection appears.

### Section 2 — "Closed / earned"

Real commissions off **funded** deals, priced on the amount **actually funded**. Grouped into
Paid → Approved → Pending → On hold → Clawback, with the same math line on every row, plus:

- the date it was paid,
- the **hold reason**, written out, if it's on hold,
- the **clawback reason**, if it was reversed.

**This page is read-only.** Questions about any row go to the owner.
