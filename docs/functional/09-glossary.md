# 9. Glossary

Terms you'll hear on day one, in the order you'll hear them.

---

## The business

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **ISO** | **Independent Sales Organization.** A brokerage that originates funding deals and submits them to funders. **That's us.** |
| **Broker** | Same thing. Used interchangeably with ISO. |
| **Funder** | The company that has the capital, underwrites the deal, and wires the money. **They pay us.** *(In the software these records are called "lenders" — same thing. On MCA deals, always say **funder** to a merchant, never "lender.")* |
| **Merchant** | The small business receiving the funding. Our customer. |
| **Sub-ISO** | A smaller broker submitting deals through **our** funder network. We keep an **override**. Supported in the platform; not in active use today. |
| **Closer** | A 1099 independent contractor sales rep who works our leads and closes deals. |

## The products

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **MCA** | **Merchant Cash Advance.** The funder **purchases a portion of the merchant's future receivables** at a discount. ⚠️ **It is NOT a loan. Never call it one.** |
| **VCF** | Our **debt-relief / restructuring** product line, for merchants buried under existing MCA debt. Runs on its own 8-stage pipeline. |
| **Term loan / SBA / Line of credit / Equipment financing** | Actual loan products. For these, standard lending vocabulary is **correct**. |

## The money

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Points** | The commission percentage the funder pays us on the funded amount. **8 points on a $50,000 deal = $4,000.** New deals **8**, renewals **6**. |
| **The pool** | The whole commission the funder pays us on a deal, before any split. Funded amount × points. |
| **Split** | The closer's share **of the pool**. Company lead **30%** (the *Momentum Standard*), self-generated **65%**, renewal **30%**. Set per closer. |
| **Momentum Standard** | Our name for the **30%** starting company-lead split. |
| **Escalator** | The step-up in a closer's company-lead split with volume: **30% → 35%** at $250K funded in a month → **40%** at $500K. Applied by the owner on the closer's record; not automatic. |
| **Draw** | A **recoverable advance against future commissions** during ramp-up — up to $2,500/month for the first 90 days. **Not a salary.** It's netted against what you earn. |
| **Clawback** | When a merchant defaults inside the funder's early window, the funder **reverses our commission** — and the closer's share is clawed back or deducted from future commissions. |
| **Override** | The points an ISO keeps on a **Sub-ISO's** deal (typically 2 of the 8). |
| **Factor rate** | The multiplier on the advance. **1.3 on $50,000 = $65,000 total payback.** Use this word — **never "interest rate."** |
| **Retrieval rate** | The daily or weekly percentage of sales debited from the merchant's account. |
| **Payback** | The total the merchant remits. Advance × factor rate. |

## The deal

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Deal** | One funding attempt for one merchant. Carries a deal number (e.g. `MF-2026-0013`), a type, a stage, an owning closer, a lead source. |
| **Stips** | **Stipulations** — the supporting documents a funder requires. Bank statements, photo ID, voided check, proof of ownership. **Chasing stips is most of the job.** |
| **Bank statements** | The last **4 months**, usually. The single biggest thing standing between you and a commission. |
| **Voided check** | Confirms the merchant's bank account. **A screenshot of their bank portal satisfies it, and it never blocks a submission.** |
| **Positions** | How many MCAs the merchant already has open. Three positions is a very different deal from zero. |
| **Stacking** | A merchant taking multiple MCAs simultaneously. Many funders refuse to fund a stacked deal — check their special instructions. |
| **Paper (A/B/C/D)** | The funder's risk tier. **A** = strong credit (700+), **B** = 600–699, **C** = 500–599, **D** = under 500 or stacked. Match the merchant to the right paper or you waste a submission. |
| **Paydown %** | How much of an existing advance the merchant has remitted. Crossing **40%** makes the deal **renewal-eligible**. |
| **Renewal** | A second advance to a merchant who's already funded and paid down. **6 points.** Submit to the incumbent funder first. |
| **Submission** | Sending a packaged deal to a funder. We send to **3–5 at once**. |
| **Submission recipe** | The per-funder configuration for how *they* want their file: where it goes, which documents, how many months of statements, their required stips, their instructions. |
| **Required stips** | Documents a specific funder **hard-requires**. Missing one blocks submission **to that funder only**. |
| **Decline** | A funder passing on the deal. Log the reason — the pattern is worth money. |
| **Offer** | A funder's terms: amount, factor rate, term, payment. **Always present 2+ to the merchant.** |

## The leads

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Lead source** | How the lead arrived. Drives which playbook opens, what colour the card is, and how fast you have to call. |
| **Live transfer** | A vendor has a pre-qualified merchant on the phone and connects them to you **live**. The most time-critical thing on the board — **15-second** SLA. |
| **Real-time lead** | A hot pre-qualified lead delivered instantly. **60-second** SLA. |
| **Aged lead** | An older lead (30+ days), sold at a discount. Re-confirm everything — the qualification is months old. |
| **Web lead (purchased)** | A lead bought from a vendor who captured them on a form. **Verify** their numbers, don't re-interrogate. |
| **UCC filing** | The public record filed when an MCA is issued. Used to find merchants who already have advances (and often want more). |
| **Speed to lead** | Seconds from the lead arriving to your first call. **The single strongest predictor of whether it closes.** |
| **Temperature** | cold → cool → warm → warmer → hot → hottest. Drives the ranking on My Day. |
| **Campaign** | A tracked lead-buying spend, with an auto-generated code like `SYN-LT-2026-001`. Every attributable KPI hangs off it. |

## The screens

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **My Day** | The ranked work queue at the top of the Revenue Playbook. Two lanes: **follow-up/chasing stips** and **new work/first touch**. |
| **Revenue Playbook** | The guided, step-by-step close for the deal you have loaded — with scripts, inline capture, and the buttons that do the real work. |
| **Deal assistant** | The AI panel that knows everything about the one deal you have open. Built to be used **mid-call**. |
| **My Earnings** | Your money: **on the table** (projection on open deals) vs. **earned** (real commissions on funded deals). |
| **Doc Review** | Where incoming merchant documents are reviewed. |
| **Funder Matrix** | Every live funder's approval criteria, side by side. |
| **Funder Guide** | Read-only: who's live, who's pending, who to route what to. |

## Compliance

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **TCPA** | The federal law governing calls and texts. Consent, quiet hours, opt-outs. Every closer signs an acknowledgment. |
| **Disclosure** | The state-mandated commercial-financing disclosure. **Different for an MCA than for a loan.** Required in several states. |
| **Broker Compensation Disclosure** | The document telling the merchant what we're paid. Goes out with every application. |
| **"No credit impact"** | True of our **application**. **Not** true of a formal submission to a funder. Say it precisely. |
| **No upfront fees** | We are **never** paid by the merchant. Only by the funder. No exceptions. |
